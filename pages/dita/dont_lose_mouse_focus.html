DITA: Avoid losing your mouse's focus during transform builds

When you initiate a bunch of transformations in OxygenXML, each time Java processes the
transform, your mouse loses focus as you see the Java icon appear in your toolbar to start the
build and then disappear when it's finished building (at least on a Mac). This can be
extremely annoying, especially if you have more than 5 or 6 transforms. You end up just
sitting there waiting until you have control with your computer again.

Fortunately, you can stop Java from taking away your mouse's focus. For DITA-OT/ANT
transforms:

  1. Edit the transformation scenario (or go to Document > Document Transformation > Configure Transformation Scenario(s) > Edit).
  2. Click the Advanced tab.
  3. In the JVM arguments field, for Macs, add a space after the first parameter, and then
    -Dapple.awt.UIElement=true. For PCs, add
    -Djava.awt.headless=true.
