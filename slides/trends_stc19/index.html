As a result, candidates with strong writing skills look to hybridize their job titles.
Technical writer/editor, Technical writer/content strategist, Technical writer/information architect, Technical writer/project manager, Technical writer/information designer.
In hiring, it's easier to check boxes for technical skills rather than evaluate writing capabilities. Which is easier to test for in hiring?" - slide: 12 title: "Developers who write as a brand" notes: "Writer shares linkedin note re being fired in favor of a developer writer." - slide: 13 title: "'Technical Technical' Writer wins job" notes: "The future is the Technical Technical Writer who knows lots of programming languages or technical frameworks. There's a growing divide between these writers and other less-technical writers." - slide: 14 title: "Why? tech getting more complex?" notes: "Tech has moved from single vendor systems to multiple vendor systems. JS frameworks have exploded. APIs interact with other APIs in ways no one tests." - slide: 15 title: "How to learn technology" notes: "How much time do you actually devote to learning technology each day? 30 min. or less." - slide: 16 title: "Learning creates a plastic mind" notes: "Brains change when we learn regularly.
