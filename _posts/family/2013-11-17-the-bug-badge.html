One of the Girl Scout badges we recently worked on was the Bug badge. This was one of the most fun badges because it involved trapping and observing all kinds of insects. We visited Rancho San Antonio park to make our bug exploration. Here are a few pictures.

[caption id=" id=" align="alignr" width="582"] The kids are running like they're avoiding a mudslide or something, but really kids just like to be active outdoors.

[caption id="attachment_475" align="alignleft" width="582"] A great place to look for bugs is a dry riverbed, under the rocks. It's moist and dark. Lots of centipedes there.

[caption id="attachment_476" align="alignleft" width="582"] Turn over rocks to see what you find. Then be ready to quickly put something in your bug house.

[caption id="attachment_477" align="alignleft" width="582"] With boxes this size, everyone can hold one.

[caption id="attachment_478" align="alignleft" width="582"] Not sure what lives in here. Reach in and find out.

[caption id="attachment_479" align="alignleft" width="582"] Molly found a couple of feathers and put them in the box.

[caption id="attachment_480" align="alignleft" width="582"] For a simple bug home, just use a plastic box and drill some holes at the top. It's much easier and more contained than trying to build a home out of a shoebox.

[caption id="attachment_481" align="alignleft" width="582"] This was a nice little butterfly... I mean moth ... that we captured and then let go.

[caption id="attachment_482" align="alignleft" width="582"] We had a pretty good time watching the bugs in here.

[caption id="attachment_483" align="alignleft" width="582"] Centipedes are some of the neatest bugs. Terrifying while also easy to catch.

[caption id="attachment_484" align="alignleft" width="582"] we put some sap we found from a tree stump in the bug box. wrong idea. One of the centipedes eventually melded with the sap. The other centipede eventually got away -- to where, we don't know.

[caption id="attachment_485" align="alignleft" width="582"] This daddy-long-leg spider only has 7 legs, which makes it part of the crane family.

[caption id="attachment_486" align="alignleft" width="582"] The rolly-polly bug must be one of my favorites -- totally harmless, easy to catch, and pretty sturdy.

[caption id="attachment_487" align="alignleft" width="582"] Callie had to make a bug poster. She chose the centipede. It turns out the centipede is a great bug for a poster since you can hang a lot of callouts off it.

[caption id="attachment_509" align="alignleft" width="580"] Here's Callie's centipede poster.

[caption id="attachment_510" align="alignleft" width="580"] A little closer up. You can't see all the details because of the flash, unfortunately.

[caption id="attachment_489" align="alignleft" width="582"] This is supposed to be a spider. You use what you have around the house.

[caption id="attachment_490" align="alignleft" width="582"] Molly likes colors so much she mixes them around until they become brownish green.

[caption id="attachment_491" align="alignleft" width="582"] We had a lot better success in making butterfly crafts instead of spider crafts.

Interestingly, when we returned from the park, we found out that we had some bed bugs in Callie and Lucy's room. We ended up disassembling their room, spreading diotomacious earth all around the room, and then attempting to clean it up. We also got protective cases for the mattresses. It took about a week to clean up. We haven't seen bed bugs in there since.
