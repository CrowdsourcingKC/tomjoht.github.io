If you're a student interested in a technical writing internship that involves working at 41st Parameter/Experian in San Jose (working closely with me), then check out the posting here.

Here's the description:

The Summer Internship Program gives students an opportunity to work at Experian's Global Fraud & ID organization. Running from late May to August, the program provides valuable real-world experience working on the 41st Parameter team in the San Jose, CA office. As an intern, you will receive first-hand exposure to the roles and responsibilities of an entry-level employee.

The technical writing internship offers the opportunity to work on technical documentation for various software products, including products with REST APIs in the digital identification space. As a technical writing intern, you will help provide user-friendly instructions on how to deploy, configure, and interact with the software.

As a technical writing intern, you will work with Markdown and HTML content integrated into a static site generator (Jekyll). You will research how systems work, gather user feedback, write help topics, edit existing content, help document internal processes, and more.

To apply, please submit your resume and a cover letter. Indicate on your cover letter and resume the position you're applying for: "Technical Writing Intern, Engineering."

The Summer 2015 Internship Program runs from May 25 - August 14, 2015 (start and end times can vary).

Qualifications

  • Good writing ability, with clear grammar and style.
  • Demonstrated interest in a technical communication career path.
  • Enrollment as an undergraduate or graduate student from an accredited institution.
  • Must be going into junior or senior year. Or be in a graduate program
  • GPA Requirement: 3.2
  • Legal authorization to work in the United States.
  • Familiarity with Markdown and HTML.

Note: The job posting is listed as a "Software Development Intern," but this is really a technical writing internship.

I want to emphasize a few things about the internship. We need someone who is really technical, who is an over-achiever, who is passionate about tech comm as a career, and who is eager to learn without too much handholding. You have to be a current student, and you have to available on site.

If you're interested, send me an email at thomas.johnson@adtruth.com. To apply, include the following:

  • Resume
  • Writing sample
  • Cover letter

The position will only be open about a week or so more. Iff you're interested, don't delay in contacting me.
